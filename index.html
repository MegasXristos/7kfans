Game Mechanics

The Pillars of Power

🗡️

Espionage & Betrayal

Infiltrate enemy villages, factories, and fortresses with spies. Let them sleep as moles — until the perfect moment to bribe a general or steal a throne.

⚖️

Diplomacy

Forge trade and military alliances, impose embargos, demand tribute, or plead for aid. Rivals remember every betrayal — and reputation decides between war and peace.

💰

Trade & Economy

Build mines, factories, and markets. Send caravans and merchant ships across the seas. Gold is the foundation of all power — master trade and you need buy no armies.

⚔️

Real-Time Battles

Command generals and soldiers in fast-paced combat. Catapults, ballistas, and cannons decide battles — as do terrain and the morale of your troops.

🔬

Science & the Gods

Research new weapons in science towers, or turn to the mystic arts. Slay a Fryhtan king, receive a scroll of power — and summon your god to the battlefield.

🌐

Multiplayer

Play against up to 6 human players over LAN or the Internet via 7kfans.com matchmaking with automatic NAT traversal. Scenarios with distinct victory conditions beyond total annihilation.

The Story of the Game

From Enlight to Open Source

Original Release

Trevor Chan and Enlight Software publish Seven Kingdoms — a unique RTS linking economy, diplomacy, and combat in one seamless experience.

1997
1998

Ancient Adversaries

The expanded edition ships with new factions, terrains, and deeper AI — and becomes the definitive version of the game.

Goes Open Source

Enlight Software releases the source code under the GPL. The community takes over maintenance, bug-fixes, and multiplayer support.

2009
